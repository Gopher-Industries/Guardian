Observation 1: Random Rotation

Initial Setting

- RandomRotation(10)

What Random Rotation Means

RandomRotation is a data augmentation technique. During training, it randomly rotates input images by a certain number of degrees. This helps the model generalize better because it sees slightly different versions of the same image.

Why It Was Changed

The rotation range was reduced from 10 degrees to 5 degrees.

The reason for this change was that facial expression images are sensitive to distortion. If the face is rotated too much, the expression can become less natural and harder for the model to learn from. A smaller rotation still gives some variation, but keeps the faces closer to realistic positions.

Effect On Performance

Reducing the rotation made the training data less distorted, which likely helped the model learn facial features more clearly and improved fitting on the dataset.

Observation 2: Dropout

Initial Setting

- Dropout(0.5)

What Dropout Means

Dropout is a regularization technique used during training. It randomly turns off a fraction of neurons in a layer so the model does not rely too heavily on specific activations. This is useful for reducing overfitting.

Why It Was Changed

The dropout was first reduced from 0.5 to 0.3.

The reason for this was that 0.5 dropout was quite strong for this CNN and may have been preventing the model from learning enough useful patterns from the data. This is called underfitting.

Effect On Performance

Lowering dropout allowed the model to keep more information during training, which improved its ability to learn facial-expression features.

Final Refinement

Dropout was later tuned further to 0.2 in the current notebook version.

This gave the model even more capacity to fit the training data while still keeping some regularization, and this was part of the final version that reached 0.6262 test accuracy.

Observation 3: Learning Rate Scheduler

Initial Setting

- No learning rate scheduler

What a Learning Rate Scheduler Means

A learning rate scheduler changes the optimizer's learning rate during training. The learning rate controls how large each update step is when the model adjusts its weights.

Why It Was Changed

A ReduceLROnPlateau scheduler was added.

This scheduler lowers the learning rate when validation loss stops improving. The reason for adding it was that a fixed learning rate can become too aggressive later in training and stop the model from settling into a better solution.

Effect On Performance

The scheduler helped training become smoother and more controlled once improvement slowed down, which likely contributed to the better validation and test performance.

Observation 4: Gradient Clipping

Initial Setting

- No gradient clipping

What Gradient Clipping Means

Gradient clipping limits how large the gradients can become during backpropagation. Gradients control how much the weights change at each update.

Why It Was Changed

Gradient clipping with clip_grad_norm_ was added.

The reason for this was to prevent unstable updates, especially when gradients become too large. Large updates can make training noisy or cause the optimizer to miss better solutions.

Effect On Performance

This made training more stable and likely helped the optimizer learn more consistently across epochs.

Observation 5: Number of Epochs

Initial Setting

- 15 epochs

What Epochs Mean

An epoch is one full pass through the training dataset. More epochs allow the model to learn for longer.

Why It Was Changed

The training schedule was increased from 15 epochs to 25 epochs in the first improved version.

The reason for this was that the model still appeared to be learning after 15 epochs, so stopping too early could leave performance on the table.

Effect On Performance

The longer schedule gave the model more time to improve. In your improved run, the best validation result appeared after the original 15-epoch range, which shows that extending training helped.

Final Refinement

In the current notebook, training is set to 20 epochs with early stopping.

This keeps the benefit of longer training while avoiding unnecessary epochs if validation performance stops improving.

Observation 6: Validation Tracking

Initial Setting

- Only training loss and training accuracy were printed during training

What Validation Tracking Means

Validation tracking measures performance on unseen data during training. It helps show whether the model is truly generalizing, not just memorizing the training set.

Why It Was Changed

Validation loss and validation accuracy were added to the training loop.

The reason for this was that training accuracy alone is not enough to judge model quality. A model can improve on training data while not improving on unseen data.

Effect On Performance

This made it possible to identify the best-performing epoch based on validation results, which directly improved model selection.

Observation 7: Checkpoint Saving

Initial Setting

- The model was saved every epoch
- In practice, this meant the final saved model was simply the last epoch

What Checkpointing Means

Checkpointing means saving the model weights during training so they can be loaded later.

Why It Was Changed

Checkpoint saving was changed so that the notebook saves the model only when validation loss improves.

The reason for this was that the last epoch is not always the best epoch. Sometimes validation performance peaks earlier and then starts to degrade.

Effect On Performance

This ensured that the final evaluated model was the best version seen during training instead of just the final one. That likely played a major role in the improvement from 0.5913 to 0.6262.

Observation 8: Early Stopping

Initial Setting

- No early stopping

What Early Stopping Means

Early stopping ends training when validation performance has stopped improving for a set number of epochs.

Why It Was Changed

Early stopping was added in the later version of the notebook.

The reason for this was to avoid wasting time on extra epochs once the model had already peaked, and to reduce the chance of overtraining.

Effect On Performance

This made the training process more efficient and kept the longer training schedule under control.
